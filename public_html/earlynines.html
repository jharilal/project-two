Internet for the General Public

The government was finally taken out of government hands and was publicly available. This allowed it to be used for commercial purposes and began the era of eCommerce. This together with the brilliant invention of W3C by Tim Burners-Lee would set the scene for the upcoming development of today's big tech companies.

Rise of Mosaic

In 1993, Marc Andreesson developed the world's first internet browser, Mosaic. The internet browser in conjunction with the availability of the internet to the public inspired a boom of users to use the internet within the coming years. Even though there were only 50 websites available to the public, Mosaic allowed the internet to be a user-friendly environment, but it will soon meet fierce competition in the mid-90s.
